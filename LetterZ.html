Z Words
  • Zephyr

    • A gentle, mild breeze, often associated with spring or early summer.

    • As they sat in the garden, they enjoyed the refreshing zephyr that rustled through the leaves.
  • Zephyrous

    • Relating to or resembling a zephyr; characterized by a gentle breeze.

    • The zephyrous evening brought relief from the heat of the day, making it perfect for a leisurely walk.
  • Zenith

    • The highest point reached by a celestial or other object; the peak or culmination of something.

    • His career reached its zenith when he won the Nobel Prize for Literature.
  • Zealot

    • A person who is fanatical and uncompromising in pursuit of their religious, political, or other beliefs.

    • The zealot refused to listen to any opinions that contradicted his own rigid ideology.
  • Zoetrope

    • A device consisting of a cylinder with slots around the circumference through which a sequence of images is viewed when the cylinder is rotated, creating the illusion of motion.

    • In the early days of animation, the zoetrope was a popular toy that fascinated both children and adults.
