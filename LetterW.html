W Words
  • Whimsical

    • Playfully quaint or fanciful, especially in an appealing and amusing way.

    • She wore a whimsical hat adorned with feathers and sequins to the costume party.
  • Wraith

    • A ghost or ghostlike image of someone, especially one seen shortly before or after their death.

    • As he walked through the old mansion, he felt a chill as if a wraith was watching him from the shadows.
  • Welter

    • A large number of items in a confused or disorderly state; also used to describe tumultuous or chaotic circumstances.

    • The office was in a welter of papers and files after the sudden departure of the manager.
  • Wherewithal

    • The money or means needed for a particular purpose; often used to indicate financial resources or capability.

    • She lacked the wherewithal to afford the expensive vacation she had been dreaming of.
  • Wanderlust

    • A strong desire or impulse to travel and explore the world.

    • Ever since she was a child, she had felt a deep wanderlust, always yearning to visit new places and experience different cultures.
