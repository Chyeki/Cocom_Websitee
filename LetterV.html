V Words
  • Vicissitude

    • Refers to a change of circumstances or fortune, typically one that is unwelcome or unpleasant.

    • The vicissitudes of life brought both joy and sorrow, shaping her into the person she is today.
  • Vestige

    • A trace or remnant of something that is disappearing or no longer exists.

    • The crumbling ruins were the only vestige of the once-great civilization that had thrived there.
  • Verdant

    • Green with grass or other rich vegetation; also used metaphorically to describe something inexperienced or new.

    • The verdant meadows stretched as far as the eye could see, bathed in the golden light of the setting sun.
  • Vicarious

    • Experienced in the imagination through the feelings or actions of another person.

    • Watching her daughter compete in the Olympics allowed her to experience vicarious joy and pride.
  • Vicennial

    • Happening every twenty years.

    • The town celebrated its vicennial anniversary with a grand parade and fireworks display.
