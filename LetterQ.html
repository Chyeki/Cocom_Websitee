Q Words
  • Quixotic

    • Quixotic describes someone who is exceedingly idealistic, often to the point of being impractical or unrealistic.

    • His quixotic quest for perfection often led him to pursue impractical or unrealistic goals.
  • Quandary

    • Quandary refers to a state of uncertainty or confusion, especially when faced with a difficult choice.

    • Faced with a difficult decision, she found herself in a quandary, unsure of which path to choose.
  • Querulous

    • Querulous describes someone who is habitually complaining or expressing dissatisfaction, often in a whiny or petulant manner.

    • The querulous customer complained about every aspect of the service, no matter how minor.
  • Quintessential

    • A quintain is a target used in jousting, consisting of a wooden arm or shield mounted on a pivot.

    • The picturesque village was the quintessential English countryside scene, complete with thatched cottages and rolling hills.
  • Quintain

    • Pulchritude refers to physical beauty, especially in a woman.

    • In medieval times, knights practiced their jousting skills by charging at a quintain, a rotating target mounted on a post.
