S Words
  • Sagacious

    • Having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment; wise.

    • The sagacious old man offered wise advice to the young couple.
  • Solecism

    • A grammatical mistake in speech or writing; a breach of etiquette or manners.

    • His frequent use of double negatives was seen as a solecism in formal writing.
  • Sempiternal

    • Enduring forever; eternal.

    • Their love seemed to exist in a sempiternal bond that transcended time.
  • Serendipity

    • The occurrence of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way.

    • Their meeting at the bookstore was a serendipitous encounter that led to a lifelong friendship.
  • Sibilant

    • Characterized by or making a hissing sound, like "s," "sh," or "z."

    • The snake made a sibilant sound as it slithered through the grass.
